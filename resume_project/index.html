Politico
Lobbyists exploit massive loophole to wine and dine lawmakers, aides at fancy getaways

I was a lead data reporter on this nine-month investigation into privately funded congressional travel. We analyzed more than 17,000 trips to reveal the top groups spending millions for members of Congress to travel the world.

Using OSINT, tax records and lobbying disclosures, I built a database linking nonprofit board members to their lobbying histories. My reporting uncovered the key finding of this investigation: the myriad of loopholes lobbyists use to get around House ethics rules limiting their involvement in congressional travel.

How the Harris campaign is galvanizing young voters in Maryland

I saw a lot of enthusiasm for the Harris campaign in the weeks after she announced her bid for president, especially from young voters. I wanted to see how that bore out in voter registration data. Some people need abortions later in pregnancy – many come to Maryland

While reporting on out-of-state abortion patients in Maryland, we found that many patients were being pushed later into pregnancy as they struggled to get an abortion within their state’s legal time limit. Reporting Tools and Workflows

Street Sense Media
Scheduled Evictions in Washington, D.C.

I created this tool to help reporters cover housing and evictions in Washington, D.C. The Office of the Tenant Advocate posts PDFs of scheduled evictions each month, which are difficult to analyze and access in aggregate. To address this, I built a web scraper to automatically check for new PDFs weekly, clean the data, add it to a running database and send me an alert.

Then I developed a Flask-based application that allows reporters to look at evictions by ward, filter for specific dates or timeframes, search by address and compare eviction rates by ward alongside Census data on race, median income, and other demographics. The tool also displays a heat map to identify eviction hotspots in the city.

I had never written Python code before starting this project, which also served as a crash course in web scraping and building web applications. I'm currently expanding this into a public-facing tool so readers can explore the geography of eviction in the city. I’m also using property records data and landlord violations to understand how demographics such as race and income and corporate ownership of homes in D.C. impacts eviction and landlord violation rates.

Skills/Tools: Web scraping (Python), Data cleaning (Python), Flask-based web app, SQLite database management
